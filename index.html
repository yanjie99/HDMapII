Abstract

High definition (HD) maps play a very important role in the realization of autonomous driving technology. It assists self-driving vehicles to efficiently and safely complete a series of driving decisions and route planning by virtue of having most of the accurate and reliable prior information in the road environment. With the continuous change of technology, there are higher requirements for the accuracy, richness and freshness of the information stored in the HD map, so as to assist the practical application of automatic driving technology. However, current research related to HD maps mainly focuses on static information in the road environment. Since there is a large amount of complex, variable and uncertain dynamic information in the road environment, it can be used as prior knowledge for self-driving to make better decisions. Therefore, the research focus of this paper is on the dynamic information. We propose to use HD map as an information system - high definition map information system (HDMIS) - to assist autonomous driving vehicles. We design the specific content of dynamic information in the HDMIS, and develop an information interaction approach based on the vehicle end of the self-driving car and the HDMIS cloud as the interactive subject of dynamic information. In the experiment, we design and build three types of specific traffic scenarios on the simulation platform, and verify the effectiveness of the interaction approach by using the database to perform information interaction between different ports. The results show that our interaction approach can meet the storage and release of dynamic information by HDMIS to a certain extent, and can provide a large amount of dynamic information for autonomous vehicles to help them complete subsequent driving decisions and planning.

Autonomous Driving HD Map Information Interaction

Autonomous driving technology relies heavily on high-definition maps to navigate complex environments. These maps provide detailed information about the road, including lane markings, traffic signs, and other important features. By leveraging HD maps, autonomous vehicles can make more informed decisions, improving safety and efficiency on the road.

The interaction between HD maps and autonomous driving systems involves continuous updates and real-time data processing. Vehicles equipped with advanced sensors collect data about their surroundings, which is then compared to the HD maps to ensure accuracy. This interaction enables the vehicle to adapt to changes in the environment, such as road construction or temporary obstacles.

One of the key challenges in autonomous driving is maintaining the accuracy and reliability of HD maps. This requires a robust infrastructure for data collection, processing, and distribution. Companies in the autonomous driving industry are investing heavily in developing and maintaining this infrastructure to support the widespread adoption of autonomous vehicles.
